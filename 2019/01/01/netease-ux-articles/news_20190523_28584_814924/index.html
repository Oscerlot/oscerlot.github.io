Introduction to Public Opinion Research
1 Jan, 2019

Machine Translated from Chinese to English
Original article written by ThunderFire UX (NetEase)

In previous work, we introduced lab testing. This issue mainly introduces another qualitative research that we often encounter in our daily work: public opinion research.

Academically speaking, public opinion is the sum of beliefs, attitudes, opinions, and emotions expressed by a large number of people about various phenomena and problems in society. The large amount of information we encounter every day, such as Weibo, WeChat public accounts, and Tieba posts, all belong to public opinion. When these comments are related to our games, they are the objects of our research.

Compared with other research methods, public opinion can quickly and agilely obtain player feedback, and has great advantages in timeliness and cost.

Game public opinion generally focuses on hot spots, problem points, and spreading points, and will be emphasized according to the different stages of the game. The game testing period, launch period, and major update period are the risk periods of public opinion. The public opinion research work at this stage will mainly monitor channel ratings, monitor negative comments, monitor spread content, and mine highlights, etc. The purpose is to assist in maintaining the gameâ€™s reputation and subsequent product improvements. During daily operations, public opinion is relatively stable. Research focuses on monitoring game popularity, collecting activity feedback, and conducting UGC content analysis. The purpose is to help evaluate the effectiveness of activities and supplement data research in the game.

In actual work, the first step is to find the right channels. The channels we generally contact include forums (such as Tieba), distribution channels (such as the App Store), social platforms (such as Weibo), and video websites (Bilibili), etc. These channels have their own strengths in terms of group typicality, content relevance, external influence, and communication power, and can generally be screened according to the focus of public opinion research. For example, as an official channel, forums are an important channel for players to feedback game experience problems, and the game relevance is high, so you can mainly pay attention to forums when monitoring gameplay experience feedback on a daily basis.

The subsequent public opinion collation work is relatively simple and has a low threshold for getting started, but making a public opinion report that is useful to the product still requires in-depth understanding of the product and advanced training in public opinion analysis skills.
