Introduction

Illustration, as an art form, with its intuitive imagery and profound appeal, can attract the attention of a wide range of consumers in commercial applications, and also has strong communication power.

Therefore, illustrations are widely used in advertising, posters, product packaging, web pages and other platforms, such as Monument Valley, the packaging of Cha Pi (茶π), and Ziroom’s splash screen. Against this background, flat illustration emerged as a relatively popular illustration style.

Next, we will understand the principles and production of flat illustration, roughly learn and grasp the main points, and create our own flat illustrations.

What is Flat Illustration?

Flat illustration is a non-realistic illustration technique that summarizes the outline of an object, extracts its features, and retains the original recognizability of the object. It is generally drawn by combining and changing basic geometric shapes (ellipses, circles, triangles, rectangles, etc.).

Flat illustration has the advantages of high versatility (wide application), easy to get started, short production time, and good effect, and the picture is simple, the subject is clear, and the “information” to be displayed can be highlighted. Therefore, it is highly praised by illustrators and designers.

For example, in the mobile game UNO!, the map part of the “World Tour” mode is a typical example of the application of flat illustration.

Because the overall game style is relatively clean and simple, it is not suitable for realistic depictions with too many details. In this case, simply flattening the photo (landmark building) can achieve the effect of retaining regional characteristics and perfectly integrating with the game, and the picture can also be appropriately beautified according to needs.

Flat Illustration Deconstruction

Since flat illustration is basically composed of simple geometric shapes, complex illustrations can be deconstructed into combinations of multiple geometries.

The following uses a few simple icon examples to illustrate the basic expression forms of flat illustration.

① Solid Color Blocks

The basic shape is obtained through Boolean operations, and simple color blocks are laid flat.

 

 

② Lines

Use lines to shape the outline

 

③ Gradient

Also on the basis of color blocks and lines, gradient processing is performed on the color blocks to increase details.

 

④ Color Overlay

Use methods such as reducing opacity to achieve a rich picture effect through color overlay contrast.

 

⑤ Texture

Flat texture is a very popular style.

It aims to use grain effects, textures, etc. to enhance the texture of the illustration and increase the durability of the picture.

Grain texture effects can be used to add a sense of space to the foreground and background, or to add shadows and highlights.

Getting Started

Recommended tools for beginners here include AI, PS, drawing tablets, etc.

The steps for beginners can be divided into copying, semi-original, and original.

 

① Copying

Copying is the simplest.

What we have to do is find a picture we like and copy a picture as similar as possible next to it.

This can familiarize you with the operation of the software, such as shortcut keys, some small skills, etc.; on the other hand, by copying, you can roughly understand the completion process of a picture and the expression of professional illustrators.

② Semi-original

The semi-original here refers to taking a photo and performing simple artistic processing on it.

It should be noted that if the final image is to be used commercially, then you must pay attention to copyright issues. Therefore, the photos we choose should meet the following two conditions:

One is that the subject of the photo has no copyright, such as a roadside rose;

The second is that the photo is preferably taken by yourself. If it is someone else’s photograph, be sure not to pick those photos with special shooting angles, as this type of photo also belongs to the author’s creation.

In addition, for the selection of photos for secondary creation, it is recommended that beginners choose front or full-side views as much as possible. In this way, there is less interference from perspective when drawing, and try to choose things with clean outlines to reduce the difficulty of outline extraction.

③ Original

After mastering the operation of copying and semi-original, it is very simple to create original works~

Just find a few more photo materials for reference, and then choose different content for combination and creativity, and it will be your own original work~

Conclusion

The above is the idea process of getting started with flat illustration.

First of all, you must have a certain understanding of flat illustration, including understanding what flat illustration is and what its expression techniques are.

Then it is to deconstruct the object information and summarize it with geometry as much as possible.

It is strongly recommended that beginners must copy a lot when getting started, and summarize the rules of extracting object outlines, that is, how to better express object characteristics with geometry.

In the process of copying, you must proceed step by step, starting with simple icons, and then to complete illustrations; when you have accumulated a certain amount, you can start to try semi-original or original.

By then, you have accumulated a lot of materials in your mind, and it will be easier to draw.

I hope everyone can draw beautiful illustrations~
