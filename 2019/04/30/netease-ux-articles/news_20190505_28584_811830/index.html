The IXDC International Experience Design Conference is one of the most prestigious design conferences in the industry. Every year, it invites many industry leaders and experts to share cutting-edge design achievements and the latest design cases through keynote speeches and workshops. As a newbie in the design industry, I have long been yearning for this conference. Last year, I finally had the honor to participate for the first time. During the 4-day conference, I gained a lot. Below I will share some of my gains from this conference.

Talking About Design Questioning

The first workshop I attended was presented by Li Yue, a senior user experience designer at Didi Chuxing’s CDX, titled “Making Good Use of the [Experience + Data Dual-Drive Model] to Derive Good Design”. It mainly used two cases to illustrate how to combine one’s own work and experience preferences to build a design thinking model suitable for oneself, and how to use the design model to guide the design and derive more effective design solutions. I think the biggest gain from this is not the design model itself, but the thinking about the essence of design and the most important and easily overlooked point in the design process: design questioning.

At the beginning, Mr. Li introduced the Double Diamond design model proposed by the British Industrial Design Association, as shown in the figure. In this course, we mainly involved the two parts in the red box.

 

A Little Story

Let’s start with a little story. A bird is flying and is very thirsty. At this time, it finds a bottle on the ground, and there is water in the bottle. How can the bird drink the water in the bottle? At that time, everyone put forward their own solutions, and almost everyone’s solutions focused on how to solve the problem of the contact point between the bird and the bottle. But if we look closely at this question, is it essentially the bird’s need?

 

As shown in the figure, through design questioning, we find that the bird’s idea of drinking water from the bottle is essentially the bird’s thirst need. Then, only by solving this essential need can the bird be most satisfied, so the path to solving the problem is broader.

Summary

In fact, in everyone’s daily life, they often directly receive the needs of the product or planning side and start designing directly. This can easily lead to the later discovery that the content carefully designed by oneself is not what the demander wants, and then fall into the situation of constantly changing. In fact, this is also due to insufficient requirement mining and failure to discover the real essential needs of the demander. When encountering problems, we must ask more questions. It is not only to help the demander diagnose whether it is a pseudo-requirement, but also to understand the problem more comprehensively in this process, so as to determine the design goal and complete the design that satisfies both parties.
