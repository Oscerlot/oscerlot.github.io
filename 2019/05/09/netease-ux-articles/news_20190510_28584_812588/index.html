Observations from Overseas Testing in the United States

Last time we shared our observations from player testing in Japan. This time, we want to share some observations from testing in the United States. For this test, we chose Los Angeles as the location, and the testing requirements were to conduct a novice test and a UI interface test for a game.

Test Preparation

Upon receiving the request, I felt a bit nervous. Testing novices? Does that mean we need to find a good coffee shop and prepare some ‘Fat Otaku Happy Water’ (soda, a common term for sugary drinks in some gaming communities.) so that foreigners can sit comfortably and play games? Wouldn’t that mean we have to start making appointments with players in China?

After some intense brainstorming, we finally decided to conduct the test in the form of lab testing plus street interviews. There are strictly controlled lab tests, as well as random interviews as a supplement to the results.

Do you think we went to Universal Studios, Santa Monica Beach to eat, drink, have fun, and watch bikini beauties? No~~~ I only saw potential subjects all over the place.

 

For overseas street interviews, large tourist attractions and beaches are excellent places for testing, because these places not only have a large flow of people, but also provide a large number of resting places (the experience of sitting down to test will be better), not to mention people of all colors and countries from all over the world.

 

When you find tourists who have been queuing for half an hour and have nothing to do, don’t hesitate, these are the target players we can recruit.

 

Of course, there are also men, women, and children eating in restaurants.

 

Player Characteristics

So after the test, what is the difference between Americans and Japanese?

Japanese people are like this, I call them “superficial brothers” - polite and courteous in conversation, generous in scoring, but when it comes to verbal feedback and game behavior, they start to show “split personality”.

 

Americans, on the other hand, are very straightforward and have a sense of American humor. During the testing process, they will be generally more enthusiastic and willing to provide various opinions and suggestions. The completion rate of other columns and suggestion columns in the questionnaire is higher than that in China.
