Introduction to User Research Lab Testing
14 May, 2019

Machine Translated from Chinese to English
Original article written by ThunderFire UX (NetEase)

Lab testing is a common method of interacting with players in user experience research, and the feedback from player testing is more real and direct, which can facilitate product iteration and optimization. So today, let’s briefly introduce our lab testing.

 

Figure 1: Qingzhiwu Lab

We currently use two labs, the Qingzhiwu Lab and the in-house lab. The Qingzhiwu Lab is located near the Yuquan Campus of Zhejiang University, in the urban area, making it convenient for players to participate in testing.

 

Figure 2: Qingzhiwu Lab

The key point of lab testing is to invite specific players (players with certain gaming experience, demographic indicators, and game experience depth) to the lab for a longer game experience, generally limited to 2 hours, observe their game experience behavior, pay attention to players’ attitudes and feedback, and use various methods to evaluate players’ experience.

 

Figure 3: In-house Eye-Tracking Lab

Lab testing will test and iterate on the core experience of the game, the novice process experience, the interaction of key interfaces in the game, new game activities or gameplay versions, etc. It can be said that it runs through the entire process of game development to online operation.

 

Before the test, we will conduct a mock test. One person plays the role of the participant experiencing the game, and multiple people play the role of the tester to observe and use the corresponding observation sheet to fill in. After the mock execution is completed, there will be a review to optimize the testing process and the documents used for testing. Make the subsequent formal testing smoother and more professional.

 

Before the test starts, we need to ensure that the players arrive on time. We will contact the players in advance by text message and phone. The test equipment will be debugged and can be used normally during the test for the game experience, reducing interruptions and facilitating the smooth progress of our test.

 

Figure 4: Qingzhiwu Justice Online Lab Test

After the test is over, we will review the test with the product on the spot, summarize and discuss key issues. An agile feedback report will be submitted on the same day. Within three days after the end, we will also submit our detailed analysis report.
