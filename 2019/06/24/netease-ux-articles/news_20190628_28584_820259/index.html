“Consumption” has unknowingly filled our lives, and games have become an indispensable part of some young people’s consumption. The “psychology” behind it has gradually been recognized by researchers and game developers. So how does consumer psychology come about? What is consumer psychology like in games?

 

Definition of Consumer Psychology

Consumer psychology is an emerging discipline that aims to study people’s psychological activities and personality traits in the process of life consumption and daily purchasing behavior.

Why is it important to study consumer psychology?

Why aren’t your players buying your costumes and appearances anymore?

Why did I spend so much money on the game this month?

It’s the game’s anniversary, what gift should I buy for my in-game partner?

 

Almost all behaviors in the game are directly or indirectly related to consumption. Only by fully understanding the player’s motivation, interests, and cognition can we possibly predict the player’s behavior.

The Origin and Development of Consumer Psychology

According to a review published by Jia, H., Zhou, S., and Allaway, A. W. (2018) in the Journal of Consumer Behavior, research related to consumer psychology appeared as early as the 19th century, when a scholar named Gale conducted an advertising effectiveness assessment based on attention and memory. Of course, when the author searched for information, he found that Fan Li in the Spring and Autumn Period had already analyzed consumer needs and managed business with “Ji Ran’s Seven Strategies”, and Xunzi proposed that production should “nurture people’s desires and satisfy people’s needs” (“Xunzi·Li Lun”), which is about meeting people’s consumption needs; in the West, Xenophon analyzed the relationship between family income and family consumption earlier. Of course, these were some early knowledge accumulation stages that were not systematic and not a discipline. It was not until 1965 that consumer psychology was first truly called a special discipline.

In their article, they mainly used the bibliometric method (simply counting the number of literature Co-citation analysis, the number of authors Co-authorship analysis, etc.) and network analysis (Networking analysis, simply counting keywords) to sort out the origin and development of consumer psychology as a whole.

 

Among them, the number of articles in the field of consumer psychology remained fairly stable between 1942 and 1992. Later, in the 1990s, as market research emphasized the importance of loyal customers to profitability (Reicheld, 1993) and the importance of branding, branding became very competitive. Since 2006, the number of published consumer psychology articles has increased dramatically, with an average of more than 70 articles published per year. Another important trigger is the popularity of online shopping. In the era of Web 2.0, more and more people are attracted to online shopping, which has triggered important research on consumers’ responses to this new shopping model. In a sense, games are also a derivative of the Web 2.0 era.
