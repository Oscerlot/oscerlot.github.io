Why do we analyze Steam sentiment? Of course, it’s because we have a game recently launched on Steam, which is—Conqueror’s Blade! Interested friends can download and experience it, and show your support!

Conqueror’s Blade is a large-scale tactical action online game set in the Ming Dynasty. You will command your troops while fighting on the front lines, launching 15v15 siege battles; and in the vast Eurasian world, conquer cities and create an epic!

Now, let’s get to the point. Regarding the Steam sentiment part, how can we find the reviews we need? What indicators and content should we pay attention to? How reliable are these indicators? And how to read and use the main reviews?

First of all, how do we find the reviews we need? Currently, the reviews are located at the bottom of our product store page. There is a filter box here, sorted by result, by language, by purchase method, and by display score. Filter out the required reviews according to your own needs, and then analyze and summarize them, so that the effect is good.

Then there is the overall rating of the game by players on Steam. The current rating has some text descriptions and also the favorable rating. As shown in the figure below, the current positive rating accounts for 97%, and the text description is “Overwhelmingly Positive.”
