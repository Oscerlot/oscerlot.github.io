[UI Sharing] Three Basic Design Principles for Gesture Interaction in Mobile Games
4 Dec, 2019

Machine Translated from Chinese to English
Original article written by ThunderFire UX (NetEase)

Gesture interaction generally appears on the touch screens of various mobile devices. Mobile devices have become one of the important terminals for people to participate in entertainment games, and gesture interaction design for touch screens has become an important design topic in the field of game design.

How to design gesture interaction with higher usability? In practice, we have summarized three important design principles for gesture interaction.

  1. Gesture interaction needs to be simple and direct. Simple gesture operation design allows players of different ages to learn at a glance, thereby stimulating players’ game fun. For example, Fruit Ninja (2010) and Angry Birds (2011) can be completely controlled with just one finger throughout the entire game process.

 

 

  1. Gesture interaction needs to conform to common sense in life. The gesture design of the game should be as simple as possible and conform to the player’s habits in the same context in real life, and the metaphorical gestures should follow the universally recognized principles.

The interactive design of gestures in the Florence game fully reflects the respect for the cognition and experience gained by players in real life when designing gesture interactions, and uses them in gesture interaction design to reduce the cost of guiding players to learn interactive behaviors.

 

The method of turning off the alarm clock is to press the alarm clock; the method of brushing teeth is to drag the toothbrush left and right, up and down; liking and refreshing social software are the same as in daily life. In Florence, players can complete these interactive behaviors by instinct without any strong guidance or text introduction.

Combined gesture interaction requires effective guidance

Different from touch interaction, gestures such as dragging, pinching, long-pressing, or wiping belong to combined gesture interaction. Combined gesture interaction can often bring a more immersive interactive experience than a mouse or controller, that is closer to real life. For example, in the Apple system, operations such as switching screens and returning to the desktop use gestures with the touchpad.

 

 

Players and users will feel that their fingers can control the screen as they wish. However, a user who has never been exposed to these interactive behaviors needs to read these gesture tutorials in detail to understand and use these interactive behaviors.

Combined gestures in games also require detailed teaching and guidance, otherwise some players will not be able to discover these interactions we provide to players, or after accidentally discovering these operations, they will not be able to use these interactive operations correctly again.

Gesture interaction requires precise control

Touch gestures are less accurate than a mouse in terms of operation. It is difficult for players to click very small buttons or drag controls to areas with pixel-level accuracy. Therefore, when designing gesture operations, we need to pay attention to the position, size, and number of buttons, as well as confirmation and undo operations.

 

Epilogue

After completing an interaction design draft containing gesture interaction, we must verify its usability. The commonly used method is to implement a prototype based on the minimum viable solution of the design on a mobile terminal, and verify the rationality of the design through practice, research, and walkthroughs.
