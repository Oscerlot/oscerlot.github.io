Q1: Why are there so few users? Will the test results be inaccurate?

According to Nielsen’s classic theory on usability testing, 5 users can find more than 80% of usability problems. The differences between individuals are not as big as we think. For example, core usability problems can often be tested by just a few people. We invite more users to the lab, and we can indeed find more problems. But the more problems found are generally minor problems. The core problems will be repeatedly mentioned by different users.

Q2：Don’t you already have a lot of channel feedback and experience in this area?

We are not trying to replace your existing experience and information, but to supplement, improve, and iterate on this experiential information. The market and users are constantly changing, and we will adopt more methods to collect different information. For example, post-00s are now receiving market attention, and what post-00s think is definitely different from post-90s.

Q3: Will you promise players/users anything?

We will not make any promises in the process of contacting users. Moreover, when collecting player suggestions, we will also follow standard procedures to tell players that we will truthfully convey the player’s suggestions, but whether to implement them requires comprehensive consideration. User researchers have undergone rigorous standardized process training and are very cautious in contacting users.

Q4: I’m worried that the content will be leaked. What are your measures?

We will have users sign a confidentiality agreement and screen users. First, the test part will have detailed communication with the stakeholders. If the IP is confidential, then we will avoid mentioning IP-related issues during the testing process. Second, every user who participates in the test needs to sign a confidentiality agreement. We have a very standardized confidentiality agreement. In addition, user researchers will actively screen some users to avoid testing industry practitioners or media personnel. Finally, when user researchers deal with confidential projects, they will not handle them publicly, but by authorized members.
