Introduction

Gestalt principles are a fundamental theory in design and have been widely used in many aspects of visual design and interaction design.

Let’s take a look at Gestalt theory and how it can make design easier.

Gestalt principles include several principles. Here we mainly share four of them: similarity, proximity, closure, and continuity.

Principle Description
Similarity Elements with similar visual appearance are perceived as a group.
Proximity Elements that are close to each other are perceived as a group.
Closure Elements that form a closed shape tend to be grouped together.
Continuity Elements that have continuous or common movement direction tend to be grouped together.

The previous article has introduced the principles of similarity and proximity. This article will continue to explain the principles of closure and continuity.

Principle of Closure and Its Application

① What is the Principle of Closure?

Closure means that elements that constitute a closed shape tend to form a whole.

As shown in the figure below, we usually think there is 1 circle and 1 point, not 9 points.

 

We usually think there is 1 circle and 1 point in the picture, not 9 points.

There are three types of closure: shape closure, negative space closure, and experiential closure.

 

Three Types of Closure

A very classic example can illustrate both shape closure and negative shape closure.

In the figure below, we can identify two triangles.

Connecting the black borders, the resulting triangle is shape closure.

Connecting the outlines of the three black circle depressions, the resulting white triangle is negative shape closure.

② Application of the Principle of Closure

The principle of closure is often used in visual design to increase the sense of variety.

Applying the principle of closure in interaction design can express more information with fewer elements, making the interface content simple and not redundant.

  1. Truncation Design

Truncation design, which is very frequently used in UI design, is based on the principle of closure.

How can we let users know that a list is scrollable?

For example, the list interface of “UNO!” adopts two methods - truncation and paging controls.

At the end of the list, a part is truncated. Because of closure, we will naturally imagine a complete whole below through the remaining part, so we know that we can slide down to view.

In many cases, this method can reduce the use of controls and reduce the burden of interface layout.

And the three-level tabs here, because of their small area, using controls can better attract users’ attention.

 

The list interface of “UNO!” uses truncation and paging controls

  1. Whitespace Implication

Closure can also help us with cognition in some cases.

Clever use of whitespace will not affect our cognition, but can add more possibilities to the design.

For example, the game Sky: Children of the Light uses only a very small part of the arc to represent an entire planet, reducing the burden of the picture while having a sense of mystery waiting to be explored.

 

Clever whitespace can add more possibilities to the design

  1. Experiential Closure

The common tab design is a form of experiential closure.

The older tab design used a very realistic and complete closure form, similar to the tabs of a notebook.

 

The old tab design adopted a realistic and complete closure form

With the evolution of the visual style of the Internet, people have experience in using the Internet, so simplifying it to the form of only one line can also make users understand the selected state.

People will naturally regard the tab and the corresponding content below as a closed whole through their own experience.

 

Experiential closure allows people to easily identify the simplified tab style

Principle of Continuity and Its Application

① What is the Principle of Continuity?

Continuity means that elements with continuous or common movement directions tend to form a whole.

Human vision tends to perceive continuous forms in order to connect elements together, rather than scattered fragments.

For example, the dots in the figure below will be seen as lines bending in two directions.

 

We will see the dots in the picture as lines bending in two directions

Compared with the principle of closure, continuity emphasizes the guidance of direction, rather than beginning and end.

If some parts of a graphic can be seen as connected together, it is also easy to be seen as a whole.

By finding very small commonalities, we can connect two different elements into a whole.

For example, the letter H and the leaf in the figure below are two completely different graphics.

But even so, it can still be connected into a whole through curves and leaf veins.

 

By finding very small commonalities, two different elements can be connected into a whole

② Application of the Principle of Continuity

  1. Visual Guidance

The Around the World mode of “UNO!” is a basic application case of the principle of continuity.

The icons of each chapter are arranged continuously. It is obvious that they are extending backwards and can be slid backwards.

Without the arrangement of these icons, we might just think it is a static landscape picture.

 

The arrangement of icons in the Around the World mode in “UNO!” adopts the principle of continuity

  1. Establish Connections

Continuity can also help us establish connections to different UI elements.

For example, the personal goal interface of “UNO!”.

When we complete the task on the right, there will be an animation of a badge flying to the progress bar on the left.

This form can very clearly let users know that the increase in the progress bar on the left is composed of completing the tasks on the right. Clarify the connection between them.

 

Continuity helps different UI elements establish connections

  1. Form a Whole

Continuity can also help us form a whole.

Take McDonald’s two coffee posters as an example.

The coffees on the left are arranged continuously one after another, which will guide our sight to look down one by one.

And it also integrates the principle of closure, making them look like a cup of coffee as a whole.

The picture on the right is an arc-shaped arrangement, forming a feeling of C, which is the first letter C of coffee.

 

 

Both coffee posters adopt the principle of continuity

  1. Space Construction

Sometimes continuity can also construct some invisible lines, and these invisible lines can help us create a sense of space.

For example, the poster of “Call of Juarez: The Cartel”.

The guns of these people are pointing at others in a staggered manner, which will give us a feeling of lines crossing in space.

This can very well increase the sense of space and dramatic conflict of this static poster.

 

The principle of continuity constructs “invisible lines” and creates a sense of space

Sometimes in UI design, we can also use the orderly arrangement between elements to construct some invisible lines.

For example, the figure below is a concept design drawing of “Anthem”.

No borders or backplanes are used here.

Instead, it relies only on the alignment and continuous arrangement of text elements to form invisible lines.

This outlines the effect of 3D UI, which is very spatial and futuristic.

It also helps us focus our sight on the image in the middle.

 

The principle of continuity increases the futuristic sense of the picture and helps focus the sight

Conclusion

In addition to the four principles shared above, Gestalt principles also include simplicity, symmetry, common fate, experience principles, etc., and also have a wide range of applications.

As a basic design theory, Gestalt’s principles are actually very simple, and have been applied in many designs.

So why do we still need to understand these?

In many cases, we judge what kind of design is reasonable based on our own experience, but we cannot say the reason for this design.

Gestalt theory can provide a basis and guidance for our design, and give us a thinking framework;

On the other hand, it also provides a basis for us to elaborate and explain our own solutions, making our solutions appear more reasonable.
