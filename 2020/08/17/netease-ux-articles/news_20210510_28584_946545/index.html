The interest curve was proposed by Jesse Schell in The Art of Game Design: A Book of Lenses.

In games, movies, or performances, interest curves are often used to record the changes in audience or player interest over time.

The novice guide of a game, in addition to letting players understand the basic rules of the game, is also the “face” of a game to attract new players’ interest.

Therefore, we can start from the perspective of the interest curve to analyze how to make the game novice guide quickly capture the player’s heart in a short time.

01 The Concept of the Interest Curve

We use the horizontal axis of the coordinate system to represent time and the vertical axis to represent the degree of interest.

According to Jesse’s theory, a good interest curve template will show the following form:

 

Example of a good interest curve in “The Art of Game Design: A Book of Lenses”

The degree of interest at point A represents people’s first impression of a segment, which is often influenced by rumors, advertisements, and other pre-promotion.

It quickly reaches point B.

Point B, as an exciting “hook” for the audience, gives the audience a happy signal and makes the audience look forward to the next paragraph.

After point B, the audience’s emotions gradually stabilize, and at point C, it reaches the peak of a paragraph.

The stability of point D then sets the stage for the next peak at point E.

At point F, it enters a gentle transition period again.

Finally, it reaches point G, reaching the climax of the entire segment, and ends the entire segment at point H.

As shown below, Jesse also mentioned two examples of interest curves that need to be improved.

 

Two examples of less-than-ideal interest curves

In Figure A, the highest point of interest does not appear at the end of the segment, which will give the audience a feeling of anticlimax.

In Figure B, there is a long period of dullness between two points of interest, which may easily cause the loss of audience or players during this process.

02 Examples of the Application of Interest Curves

Similar application cases of interest curves can be found in many works of art.

Let’s take the performance of “Big Bowl Thick Noodle” in the variety show “Sisters Who Make Waves” as an example:

There are 4 sections of opera singing in the whole performance, all of which are highlights.

If we arrange them according to the timeline, we can get the following interest curve:

 

The distribution of opera singing and the interest curve of “Big Bowl Thick Noodle (Sister Lang Version)”

At the beginning of the performance, Yu Kewei’s opera singing opening suddenly raised the audience’s expectations.

Then the two sections of opera singing by Zhang Hanyun are also the focus of everyone.

Finally, it is still Yu Kewei’s singing, accompanied by the chorus of other sisters, pushing the entire performance to the highest climax.

Similarly, this curve is also applied in many three-act movies, such as “The Shawshank Redemption”.

In the cause, process, and result stages of the movie, we can all see several plots that can stimulate the audience’s strong interest.

It is under the continuous advancement of “foreshadowing-climax-foreshadowing-climax-…” that the audience’s attention can closely follow the fate of the protagonist.

 

The plot interest curve of the movie “The Shawshank Redemption”

03 The Composition of Interest

Since we want to arouse interest, we must understand what constitutes interest.

In “The Art of Game Design: A Book of Lenses”, three main components of interest are mentioned:

(1) Inherent interest

Adventure, fantasy, specials, and reward mechanisms are inherently interesting.

(2) Artistic expression

That is, aesthetics in the game.

(3) Immersion

When players have a strong empathy for the tasks in the game, interest also arises.

Next, let’s talk about these three constituent points in detail with specific examples in the game.

1. Inherent Interest

In “Uncharted: The Lost Legacy”, the protagonist needs to go through a series of level challenges.

For example, jumping with the help of billboards, lurking with the help of cover, sliding on tall buildings, etc.

A certain degree of difficulty and thrilling pictures are already interesting enough for players.

In addition, there will be some calm and soothing passages between several levels, such as the dialogue between two characters, to avoid the appearance of interest points being too dense.

 

In “Uncharted: The Lost Legacy”, the protagonist jumps between billboards

 

In “Uncharted: The Lost Legacy”, the protagonist jumps onto the high wall and slides on the electric wire

In “Luigi’s Mansion”, a series of fright events and novel and fun interactive methods also give players a very strong interest.

While the player thinks the game is fun, they also memorize the game operation of using the flashlight to shine around.

 

In “Luigi’s Mansion”, Luigi opens the closet and is scared by the bouncing toy

 

In “Luigi’s Mansion”, the combination of vacuum cleaner and flashlight can pull objects closer or push them away

In the novice tutorial of “UNO Together”, the player narrowly defeats the system opponent with the advantage of one card.

This arrangement greatly stimulates the player’s interest in the game and gives the player a deeper impression of the game.
